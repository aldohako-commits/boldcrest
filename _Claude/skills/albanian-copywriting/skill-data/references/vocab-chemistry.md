## Element & Compound Naming in Albanian

### Albanian Adaptation of IUPAC Nomenclature

Albanian chemistry terminology follows IUPAC conventions with specific Albanian morphological patterns:

#### **Organic Compound Naming Patterns**

**Alkanes (Alkanet):** `[prefix] + -an`
- metane (methane)
- etane (ethane)
- propane (propane)
- butane (butane)
- pentane (pentane)
- heksane (hexane)
- heptane (heptane)
- oktane (octane)
- noname (nonane)
- dekane (decane)

**Alkenes (Alkenet):** `[prefix] + -en`
- eteni (ethene)
- propeni (propene)
- buteni (butene)
- penteni (pentene)
- hekseni (hexene)

**Alkynes (Alkinë):** `[prefix] + -in`
- etini (ethyne/acetylene)
- propini (propyne)
- butini (butyne)

**Alcohols (Alkoolet):** `[prefix] + -ol`
- metanol (methanol)
- etanol (ethanol)
- propanol (propanol)
- butanol (butanol)

**Aldehydes (Aldehidet):** `[prefix] + -aldehid`
- metanaldehid (methanal/formaldehyde)
- etanaldehid (ethanal/acetaldehyde)
- propanaldehid (propanal)
- butanaldehid (butanal)

**Carboxylic Acids (Acidet karboksilike):** `[prefix] + -oik acid`
- acid etanoik (acetic acid)
- acid propanoik (propionic acid)
- acid butanoik (butyric acid)
- acid benzoik (benzoic acid)
- acid etandioik (oxalic acid)

**Ketones:** `[prefix] + -on`
- propanon (acetone)
- butanon (butanone)
- pentanon (pentanone)

#### **Inorganic Compound Naming**

**Chlorides (Klorurët):** `[element name] + -ur`
- klorur natriumi (sodium chloride, NaCl)
- klorur kalciumi (calcium chloride, CaCl₂)
- klorur bariumi (barium chloride, BaCl₂)

**Bromides (Bromurët):** `[element name] + -ur`
- bromur natriumi (sodium bromide)
- bromur kalciumi (calcium bromide)

**Iodides (Jodurët):** `[element name] + -ur`
- jodur potasiumi (potassium iodide)

**Oxides (Oksidet):** `[element name] + -id`
- oksid karboni (carbon dioxide, CO₂)
- oksid mangani (manganese oxide)
- oksid magnesiumi (magnesium oxide)
- oksid kalciumi (calcium oxide, quicklime)

**Hydroxides (Hidroksidet):** `[element name] + -id` or separate "hidroksid"
- hidroksid natriumi (sodium hydroxide, NaOH)
- hidroksid potasiumi (potassium hydroxide, KOH)
- hidroksid kalciumi (calcium hydroxide, Ca(OH)₂)

**Carbonates (Karbonatët):** `[element name] + -at`
- karbonat kalciumi (calcium carbonate, CaCO₃)
- karbonat natriumi (sodium carbonate, Na₂CO₃)

**Sulfates (Sulfatët):** `[element name] + -at`
- sulfat bakri (copper sulfate, CuSO₄)
- sulfat magnesiumi (magnesium sulfate, MgSO₄)

**Nitrates (Nitratët):** `[element name] + -at`
- nitrat kalciumi (calcium nitrate)
- nitrat bakri (copper nitrate)

**Phosphates (Fosfatët):** `[element name] + -at`
- fosfat kalciumi (calcium phosphate)
- fosfat natriumi (sodium phosphate)

## Sentence Patterns for Scientific Writing

### Process Description Patterns

#### **How a reaction occurs:**
- "Hidrogjeni bashkëvepron me oksigjenen duke dhënë ujë."
  (Hydrogen reacts with oxygen to give water.)

- "Kur i shtojmë acid, formohet një preçipitat i bardhë."
  (When we add acid, a white precipitate forms.)

- "Në kujdeset normale, metani ndodhet në gjendje gazi."
  (Under normal conditions, methane exists as a gas.)

- "Gjatë reaksionit, nxehtësia çlirohet në mjedis."
  (During the reaction, heat is released to the surroundings.)

#### **Describing properties:**
- "Elektronegativiteti i elementeve rritet në perioda dhe zvogëlohet në grupe."
  (Electronegativity increases across periods and decreases down groups.)

- "Rrezja atomike është largësia më e vogël midis bërthamave të dy atomeve të afërt."
  (Atomic radius is the shortest distance between the nuclei of two adjacent atoms.)

- "Dendësia e metalit zvogëlohet me rritjen e temperaturës."
  (The density of the metal decreases with increasing temperature.)

#### **Causal relationships:**
- "Meqë shtresa e jashtme është e plotë, elementi është inert."
  (Since the outer shell is full, the element is inert.)

- "Për shkak të elektronegativitetit të madh, klori ka tendencë të fitojë elektrone."
  (Due to its high electronegativity, chlorine tends to gain electrons.)

- "Si rezultat i lidhjes hidrogjenore, uji ka pikë të lartë të vlimit."
  (As a result of hydrogen bonding, water has a high boiling point.)

#### **Conditional/hypothetical:**
- "Nëse përqendrimi i H⁺ zvogëlohet, pH rritet."
  (If the concentration of H⁺ decreases, pH increases.)

- "Nëse rritet temperatura, shpejtësia e reaksionit zvogëlohet."
  (If temperature increases, the reaction rate decreases.)

- "Nëse lëshojmë një elektron, atomi bëhet jon pozitiv."
  (If we remove an electron, the atom becomes a positive ion.)

#### **Comparison:**
- "Lidhja jonike është më e fortë se lidhja van der Waals."
  (The ionic bond is stronger than a van der Waals force.)

- "Alkenët janë më reaktive sesa alkanet."
  (Alkenes are more reactive than alkanes.)

- "Entalpia standarde e formimit është më e ulët për produktet sesa për reaktantët."
  (The standard enthalpy of formation is lower for products than for reactants.)

#### **Sequence & procedure:**
- "Fillimisht nxehtojmë tretjen në rreth 50°C, pastaj i shtojmë kristalet."
  (First we heat the solution to about 50°C, then we add the crystals.)

- "Para se të matim vëllimin, duhet ta zerove cilindrin matës."
  (Before measuring the volume, you must zero the measuring cylinder.)

- "Pasi të kristalijën soda, e filtroni dhe e lani me ujë të ftohtë."

#### **Key characteristics:**

1. **Passive voice preference:**
   - Tretja filtrohet. (The solution is filtered.) — preferred over

## Pedagogical Notes for Learners

### How Albanian Organizes Chemistry

1. **Compound naming is highly systematic:**
   - Unlike English ("methane, ethane, propane..."), Albanian follows strict logical patterns
   - Once you learn "metan, etan, propan," the pattern is clear for all alkanes
   - Functional groups add suffixes consistently: -ol (alcohols), -al (aldehydes), -oik (carboxylic)

2. **Scientific sentences use distinctive structures:**
   - Passive voice is dominant: "Elektronet orbitohen" (electrons orbit) not "Elektronet orbitin"
   - Nominalization is common: speak of "rritja e temperaturës" (rise in temperature) not "temperatura rritet"
   - Conditional "nëse" introduces logical relationships, not always actual conditions

3. **Albanian chemistry preserves IUPAC structure:**
   - IUPAC names are adapted but remain recognizable
   - Element names are mostly transparent (Hekuri = iron, but clearly related to hek-/iron concepts)
   - This makes it easier to reference international literature

4. **Formality markers are essential:**
   - Scientific Albanian uses distinct markers: "Sipas...", "Mund të nxirret...", "Është konfirmuar..."
   - These are not optional but signal academic register
   - Avoid colloquial forms in scientific writing

5. **Units and symbols are universal:**
   - Use always SI units: mol/L not mol per liter
   - Write "25°C" not "25 gradë Celsius"
   - Write "50 mL" not "50 millilitër"

## Grammar Context in Chemistry Texts

### 1. Gender of Chemical Terms
Chemical vocabulary maintains strict gender patterns across compounds and elements:
- **Masculine**: metali (metal), reaksioni (reaction), acidin (acid), gazin (gas), zinkun (zinc), hekurin (iron), aluminit (aluminum), oksidin (oxide)
- **Feminine**: molekula (molecule—usually), nxehtësia (heat), tretësira (solution), temperatura (temperature), shkala (scale), prova (test/experiment), përzierja (mixture)
- **Neuter-like patterns**: hidrogjenin (hydrogen), magnezin (magnesium), sulfatin (sulfate)
- **Example declensions**:
  - "metali më aktiv" (more active metal—masculine)
  - "përzierja e madhe" (large mixture—feminine)
  - "reagenti i marrë" (taken reagent—masculine)

### 2. Definite vs. Indefinite Articles in Chemical Contexts
Chemistry texts consistently use definite articles for:
- **First mention of defined concepts**: "Xeherori quhet shkëmbi..." (Ore [the concept] is called the rock...)
- **Unique substances**: "Hekuri", "Bakri" (Iron, Copper—as singular entities)
- **Repeating references**: "metali i panjohur" (the unknown metal), "radhën e aktivitetit" (the activity series)
- **Definite vs. indefinite distinction**:
  - "një reaksion ekzotermik" (an exothermic reaction—indefinite)
  - "reaksioni i bashkëveprimit" (the reaction of interaction—definite)

### 3. Passive Voice and Nominalization Patterns
Chemistry texts heavily favor passive constructions for scientific objectivity:
- **Passive infinitives**: "Për të filluar reaksioni" (For [the reaction] to start), "për të nxjerrë hekur" (to extract iron)
- **Nominalization**:
  - "zhvillimin e eksperimenteve" (conducting of experiments)
  - "përfundimin e reaksionit" (completion of reaction)
  - "çlirimin e nxehtësisë" (release of heat)
  - "formimin e produkteve" (formation of products)
- **Verbal nouns as objects**: "matin kohën" (measure time), "njehsojnë vëllimin" (calculate volume), "vrojtojnë reaksionin" (observe reaction)

### 4. Genitive Chains in Chemical Nomenclature
Complex chemical terms use cascading genitives:
- **Element genitives**: "Oksidin e hekurit", "Sulfatin e magnezit", "oksidin e aluminit", "oksidin e bakrit"
- **Compound chains**: "Sasia e nxehtësisë që nevojitet" (the amount of heat that is needed)
- **Hierarchical possession**: "Pika e shkrirjes së hekurit" (the melting point of iron), "prurjet e mëdha të lumit" (the large flows of the river)
- **Multiple genitives**: "Temperatura e acidit sulfurik" (the temperature of the sulfuric acid solution)

### 5. Adjective Agreement with Technical Nouns
Chemistry maintains strict gender and number agreement:
- **Masculine agreements**:
  - "reaksioni ekzotermik" (exothermic reaction)
  - "metali më aktiv" (more active metal)
  - "gazi i çliruar" (released gas)
- **Feminine agreements**:
  - "tretësirë ujore" (aqueous solution)
  - "përzierja e madhe" (large mixture)
  - "nxehtësia e liruar" (released heat)
- **Plural forms**:
  - "reaksionet ekzotermike" (exothermic reactions)
  - "metalin e panjohur" (the unknown metals - object form)

### 6. Imperative Forms in Experimental Instructions
Chemistry texts use a standard set of imperative verbs for procedure:
- **Common imperatives**: vrojtoni (observe), shënoni (write down), përdorni (use), hidhni (add), lajini (wash), përsëritni (repeat)
- **Infinitive imperatives**: "Për të filluar..." (To start...), "Për të parë nëse..." (To see if...)
- **Negative imperatives**: "Mos i prekni metalet" (Do not touch the metals), "Mos merren parasysh" (Do not take into account)
- **Pattern**: Imperative + adverb (carefully, well, again) + object

### 7. Temporal Markers and Sequence in Procedures
Chemistry texts use specific temporal connectors:
- **Sequential**: "fillimisht...pastaj...më pas" (first...then...later)
- **Temporal measurement**: "në 45 sekonda", "në 10 sekonda" (in 45 seconds, in 10 seconds)
- **Duration**: "për 45 sekonda u çliruan 1,8 litra" (in 45 seconds, 1.8 liters were released)
- **Causal sequences**: "pasi...atëherë" (since...then), "përderisa...deri sa" (while...until)

### 8. Comparative and Superlative Formation
Chemistry texts systematize comparisons through standard formations:
- **Comparatives**: "më aktiv se" (more active than), "më i madh se" (larger than), "më i holluar" (more dilute)
- **Superlatives**: "më aktiv" (most active—in context), "më i madh" (largest), "më pak aktiv" (least active)
- **Gradation expressions**: "aq shumë...sa..." (so much...that...), "sa më lartë...aq më i shpejtë" (the higher...the faster)

### 9. Modal Verbs and Necessity Expressions
Chemistry texts express necessity and possibility systematically:
- **Necessity**: "duhet", "nevojitet", "kërkohet" (must, is needed, is required)
  - "Për të filluar reaksioni duhet të nxehet" (To start, the reaction must be heated)
  - "Nevojitet më shumë nxehtësi" (More heat is needed)
- **Possibility**: "mund të", "është e mundur" (can, is possible)
  - "Mund të përdoret për të nxjerrë" (Can be used to extract)
- **Negation**: "Nuk mund të...", "Nuk ndodh..." (Cannot happen)

### 10. Causative Constructions ("shkakton", "çon në", "e bën të")
Chemistry texts express causation through multiple structures:
- **Direct causation**: "Rritja e temperaturës shkakton rritjen e shpejtësisë" (Temperature increase causes rate increase)
- **Indirect causation**: "Temperatura rritet aq shumë, sa hekuri përftohet në gjendje të lëngjet" (Temperature rises so much that iron is obtained liquid)
- **Process causation**: "për shkak të shtrirjes" (because of the extension), "si rezultat" (as a result)
- **Necessitative**: "Për të filluar reaksioni, përzierja duhet të nxehet" (For the reaction to start, the mixture must be heated)

### 11. International/Borrowed Chemical Terms
Chemistry extensively adapts scientific terms from English and Latin:
- **Direct borrowing with Albanian endings**:
  - ekzotermik (exothermic), endotermik (endothermic)
  - aluminotermi (aluminothermy)
  - aktiviteti (activity)
- **Hybrid formations**:
  - "sulfatin e bakrit" (copper sulfate—element name + Albanian genitive)
  - "oksidi i hekurit" (iron oxide)
- **Latin-based terms**: "horst-graben" (directional borrowing), "oksidin" (oxide—from oxygen)

### 12. Measurement Vocabulary and Quantifiers
Chemistry texts use precise measurement language:
- **Volume**: "ml", "litra" (milliliters, liters) — "u çliruan 1,8 litra hidrogjen"
- **Mass**: "g" (grams) — "2 g shirit magnezi"
- **Temperature**: "°C" (degrees Celsius) — "Temperatura rritet aq shumë"
- **Time**: "sekonda", "minuta" (seconds, minutes) — "Për 45 sekonda u çliruan"
- **Rate**: "shpejtësia" (speed/rate) — "shpejtësia e reaksionit" (reaction rate)
- **Quantifiers**: "sasi", "vëllim", "masë" (amount, volume, mass)

### 13. Conditional and Hypothetical Statements
Chemistry uses if-then structures for experimental predictions:
- **Conditional present**: "Nëse rritja e temperaturës do të rritë shpejtësinë" (If temperature increase will increase the rate)
- **Counterfactual**: "A ndodh reaksion nëse..." (Does a reaction occur if...)
- **Predictive**: "Parashikoni ç'do të ndodhë nëse Emi do të rritë temperaturën" (Predict what will happen if Emi raises the temperature)
- **Result clauses**: "aq shumë...sa që" (so much...that), "aq...sa të" (enough...to)

### 14. Relative Clauses with Chemical Substances
Chemistry extensively uses relative clauses to identify and describe substances:
- **Identifying clauses**: "Xeherori quhet shkëmbi që përmban oksidin e metalit" (Ore is the rock that contains metal oxide)
- **Descriptive clauses**: "metalet...të cilat ndeshen" (metals...which are found), "tretimet ujore të të cilave" (aqueous solutions of which)
- **Particle clauses**: "gazi i çliruar" (released gas), "produktet e përftuar" (obtained products), "temperatura e rritur" (increased temperature)

### 15. Scientific Claims and Evidence ("del se", "rezultoi se", "vutë re")
Chemistry texts make and support scientific statements:
- **Evidence-based claims**: "Del se alumini është më aktiv se hekuri" (It turns out that aluminum is more active than iron)
- **Experimental results**: "Rezultoi se për 45 sekonda u çliruan 1,8 litra" (It turned out that in 45 seconds 1.8 liters were released)
- **Observation reporting**: "Vutë re se rezultatet e njërës probe ishin jonormale" (We noticed that the results of one test were abnormal)
- **Pattern recognition**: "Bazuar në eksperimentet...mund të përfundojmë se..." (Based on the experiments...we can conclude that...)
