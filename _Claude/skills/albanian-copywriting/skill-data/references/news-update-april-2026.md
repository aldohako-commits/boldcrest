## KEY SENTENCE STRUCTURES (New or Refined)

### 1. Progressive Aspect "po" as Accusatory Habitual
**Pattern:** Subject + po + present verb (to imply habitual/ongoing failure)
**Example:** "Rama po gjen fajtore të tjere" (Rama keeps finding other culprits)
**Note:** March 2026 documented "po" for ongoing action. April 2026 reveals its accusatory use — "po gjen" doesn't just mean "is finding" but "keeps finding / habitually finds" as a character indictment. Distinctive Balkanweb pattern.

### 2. Object-Verb-Subject (OVS) Fronting in Business Headlines
**Pattern:** Object fronted before verb and subject for emphasis
**Example:** "Po zëvendësojnë portet Ngushticën e Hormuzit" (Ports are replacing the Strait of Hormuz)
**Note:** BusinessMag uses OVS more aggressively than news outlets. The fronted object creates urgency about what's being disrupted.

### 3. "Pa" Negation in Headlines for Privation
**Pattern:** "Pa" + noun = without (creates sense of systemic lack)
**Example:** "Opozita pa besim" (Opposition without credibility) / "Banka pa sportele" (Banking without branches)
**Note:** Balkanweb/Monitor use "pa" negation in headlines to suggest systemic deprivation. More emotionally loaded than "nuk ka" (doesn't have).

### 4. Quoted Emotional Exclamation as Headline Lead
**Pattern:** "Direct quote" + attribution verb + subject
**Example:** "Çmendem pas teje", Mateo i kërkon Brikenës... / "Stop bullizimit", Dafina shpërthen
**Note:** Revista Who/Anabel signature — places the most emotionally charged quote at the very beginning, before any context. Creates tabloid-style hooks.

### 5. Present Tense for Near-Future in Business
**Pattern:** Present tense replaces future for predictions/projections
**Example:** "Çfarë funksionon në 2026-ën?" (What works in 2026?) — NOT "Çfarë do të funksionojë"
**Note:** BusinessMag/Monitor use present tense for near-future business predictions, creating inevitability rather than speculation.

### 6. Rhetorical Question as Speculation in Gossip Register
**Pattern:** Statement framed as question = rumor/unconfirmed
**Example:** "Kleofina dhe Benjamin Pavard prindër për herë të parë?" (parents for the first time?)
**Note:** Revista Who uses question marks to present unconfirmed celebrity news as compelling questions, maintaining deniability while generating excitement.

### 7. Genitive Chain Stacking in Financial/Legal
**Pattern:** Noun + "i/e" + noun + "të" + adjective (double-marked genitive)
**Example:** "tregu i pasurive të paluajtshme" (market of immovable properties)
**Note:** Monitor/BusinessMag financial register stacks genitive markers creating heavy noun phrases that would be simple compounds in English.

## GRAMMAR OBSERVATIONS (New)

### 1. Evidentiality Through Aspect Choice (Monitor)
Perfective "disbursuan" (disbursed — completed fact) vs. imperfective "disbursojnë" (disburse — ongoing process). Financial journalism uses aspect to signal epistemological certainty vs. ongoing trends.

### 2. Middle Voice vs. True Passive (Cross-Outlet)
"Struktura lidhet me kosto" (Structure connects-itself with cost) — middle voice with different agency implication than passive "Struktura është lidhur me kosto" (Structure has been connected with cost). Albanian prefers middle voice in analytical writing.

### 3. Nominalization Density in Business Albanian (BusinessMag)
Suffix "-im" creates abstract nouns: investim, riciklim, financim, digjitalizim. Business Albanian is far more heavily nominalized than news Albanian. Creates formal register density.

### 4. English Loanword Gender Assignment (BusinessMag)
Borrowed terms receive inconsistent gender: "startup-i" (masculine) but invariant "startup" also used. Tech terms resist full morphological integration unlike older loanwords.

### 5. Reflexive "u-" for Agentless Passive in Announcements (Monitor/BusinessMag)
"u lancohet zyrtarisht" (was officially launched) — reflexive passive implies participation of subject in own action. Standard in official/corporate announcements.

### 6. Double Negation Scope (Cross-Outlet)
"nuk ka kaluar pa u vënë re" (didn't pass without being noticed) — Albanian stacks negations legally: nuk + pa + u. Creates emphasis impossible to achieve with single English negation "didn't go unnoticed."

### 7. Perfective vs. Imperfective in Gossip (Revista Who/Anabel)
"thyen heshtjen" (broke silence — perfective, complete) vs. "po bëhet trend" (is becoming — imperfective, ongoing). Celebrity journalism uses perfective for decisive moments and imperfective for developing stories.

- Single-name celebrity references (no surnames — audience familiarity assumed)

### Top Channel/Shqiptarja: Standard News Register (Baseline)
- "Ka + participle" present perfect continues as default news tense
- Direct quote headlines dominate
- Balanced formality — accessible but not colloquial
- Parenthetical tags more common: (VIDEO), (DETAJE), (EKSKLUZIVE)

### Gazeta Express: Kosovo Albanian Register
- Warmer, more personal narratives
- Kosovo political terminology: Kuvendi, Gjykata Kushtetuese, Asociacioni

- Slightly more emotive than Albania outlets

3. **Cybersecurity vocabulary emerged** — zero-day, sandbox, payload now appear naturally in Albanian text
4. **Progressive "po" expanded in accusatory contexts** — beyond simple ongoing action
5. **Reality TV vocabulary stabilized** — "banorë" (residents/contestants), "vulosi" (sealed) as relationship vocabulary
