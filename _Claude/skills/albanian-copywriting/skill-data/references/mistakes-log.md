## CATEGORY 1: TONE & REGISTER

### MISTAKE 1: Cold Opens Default to Educational Tone
**Frequency:** 4/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI opens with hypothetical/informational framing instead of direct confrontation.
**Why it's wrong:** Albanian persuasive copy — especially video scripts — demands confrontational energy. The audience expects to be challenged, not lectured.

- ❌ "Nëse mendoni se makina elektrike ju kursen para — le t'i shohim numrat."
- ✅ "Prit pak. Mendon që makina elektrike të kursejë para? Hajde t'i bëjmë llogaritë. TANI."

**Rule:** Open with a punch. Use "Prit" (Wait), "Hajde" (Let's go), direct "ti" address. Never open with "Nëse mendoni..." in persuasive copy.

---

### MISTAKE 2: Formal JU Form Instead of Intimate TI Form
**Frequency:** 3/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI defaults to formal plural "ju/keni" when the persona requires singular intimate "ti/ke".
**Why it's wrong:** Wolf of Wall Street / confident seller persona talks TO you, not AT a group. JU creates distance.

- ❌ "Keni makinë me benzinë? Ky është numri juaj."
- ✅ "Ke makinë me karburant dhe s'di sa po harxhon?"

**Rule:** For direct-audience persuasive copy (reels, ads, personal pitches), default to TI form unless the brief explicitly requests JU. Check every "keni/jeni/doni" — should it be "ke/je/do"?

---

### MISTAKE 3: Passive Third-Person Instead of Direct Accusation
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI externalizes objections to "dikush" (someone) instead of owning the listener's thought.
**Why it's wrong:** Saying "someone tells you X" lets the listener hide. Saying "you think X" pins them.

- ❌ "Po ju thotë dikush të blini flotë elektrike. Le ta shohim."
- ✅ "Mendoni se zgjidhja janë makinat elektrike? Ti llogarisim. TANI."

**Rule:** Never use "dikush" (someone) or "ndonjëri" (some people) when you can directly address the listener's belief. Own the confrontation.

---

### MISTAKE 4: Too Deferential in Closing
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI tells the audience to "decide for themselves" instead of owning the argument.
**Why it's wrong:** The confident persona doesn't abdicate. They state the conclusion.

- ❌ "Llogarisni vetë. Vendosni ju."
- ✅ "Aritmetikë e thjeshtë. Mos më beso mua, beso faktet. Automita."

**Rule:** Close with confidence. The persona owns the math and the conclusion. Never say "decide for yourself" — that's weakness.

## CATEGORY 2: URGENCY & PACING

### MISTAKE 5: Missing "TANI" (NOW) Urgency Marker
**Frequency:** 5/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI scripts lack the single-word "TANI" punctuation that signals immediate decision-making.
**Why it's wrong:** "TANI" is the rhythm engine of Albanian persuasive video. It creates urgency and signals the start of proof.

- ❌ "Hajde t'i bëjmë llogaritë."
- ✅ "Hajde t'i bëjmë llogaritë. TANI."

**Rule:** In persuasive Albanian video copy, always include at least one "TANI" per reel. Place it after the hook or the pivot to calculations.

---

### MISTAKE 6: Missing "Hajde" Conversational Marker
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI uses formal constructions where "Hajde" (let's go / come on) would inject energy.
**Why it's wrong:** "Hajde" is the Albanian conversational accelerator. It signals "we're doing this together, right now."

- ❌ "Le t'i shohim numrat."
- ✅ "Hajde t'i bëjmë llogaritë."

**Rule:** Use "Hajde" + subjunctive for every "let's do X" moment. "Le" is literary; "Hajde" is spoken energy.

---

### MISTAKE 7: Missing Visual Beat / Pause Notation
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI doesn't mark pauses, eye-contact moments, or gesture cues in some reels.
**Why it's wrong:** Broadcast copy needs rhythm marks. Without them, the delivery is flat.

- ❌ Wall of text with no performance direction
- ✅ "(pauzë, shikon drejt kamerës)" / "(troket lehtë tavolinën)"

**Rule:** Every reel script must include at least 2-3 performance cues: pauses, gestures, camera direction. Write for the performer, not the reader.

## CATEGORY 3: SCRIPT STRUCTURE

### MISTAKE 8: Production Notes in Dialogue
**Frequency:** 3/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI embeds "sipas Kalkulime.xlsx" directly in the spoken script.
**Why it's wrong:** "Kalkulime.xlsx" is a production reference. It breaks immersion when spoken aloud. The audience doesn't care about your spreadsheet filename.

- ❌ "Nëse mendoni se makina elektrike ju kursen para — le t'i shohim numrat sipas Kalkulime.xlsx."
- ✅ Script contains the spoken argument; caption/footer references the source.

**Rule:** NEVER embed filenames, source references, or production notes in spoken dialogue. These go in captions, graphics, or footnotes.

---

### MISTAKE 9: Over-Explaining Math in Spoken Script
**Frequency:** 3/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI puts full calculation breakdowns in the dialogue instead of letting graphics carry the numbers.
**Why it's wrong:** A 40-60 second reel can't carry a lecture. Detailed math belongs on screen, not in speech.

- ❌ Spoken: "20 herë 0.40 euro per kilovat-ore = 8.00 euro per 100 kilometra"
- ✅ Spoken: States the result. Graphic: Shows the calculation.

**Rule:** In video scripts, the spoken word delivers the argument and the punch line. The numbers and proof go in on-screen graphics. Separate spoken argument from visual proof.

---

### MISTAKE 10: Missing Consistent Tagline
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI ends scripts with different closings or no branded tagline.
**Why it's wrong:** Campaign consistency requires a single, repeated branded close.

- ❌ Different closings per reel, or ending with "DM: FLOTE — automita.al"
- ✅ "Mos më beso mua, beso faktet. Automita." (or variant per brief)

**Rule:** Every piece in a campaign series must close with the same branded tagline unless the brief explicitly varies it.

## CATEGORY 4: GRAMMAR & SPELLING

### MISTAKE 11: Diacritic Inconsistency ("per" vs "për")
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI writes "per" (non-standard) instead of "për" (standard Albanian).
**Why it's wrong:** "Për" is the correct preposition. "Per" without the diacritic is not standard Albanian.

- ❌ "per kilovat" / "per 100 km"
- ✅ "për kilovat" / "për 100 km"

**Rule:** Always use "për" with the ë diacritic. Scan every output for "per " and replace with "për ".

---

### MISTAKE 12: Past Tense for Habitual Present
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI uses past tense "prishte" (it broke) when making a general claim that needs present habitual "prishet" (it breaks).
**Why it's wrong:** A generalized claim about converted gas systems breaking needs present tense, not past.

- ❌ "Kur prishte — garancia nuk e mbulon." (When it broke...)
- ✅ "Dhe kur prishet... GARANCIA nuk e mbulon." (When it breaks...)

**Rule:** For general truths and habitual claims in persuasive copy, always use present tense. Past tense is only for specific past events.

---

### MISTAKE 13: Dangling Conditionals
**Frequency:** 1/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI writes "Nëse nuk rritet Akoma" (If it doesn't rise yet) — an incomplete thought.
**Why it's wrong:** The sentence starts a condition but never completes it. The listener is left hanging.

- ❌ "2 euro litri. Nëse nuk rritet Akoma."
- ✅ "2 euro litri. Nëse nuk rritet akoma." (integrated as parenthetical, or complete the condition)

**Rule:** Every "Nëse..." must have a resolution. If it's a parenthetical ("if it doesn't rise yet"), integrate it into the previous sentence with a dash or comma.

---

### MISTAKE 14: Genitive Case Drop
**Frequency:** 1/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI writes "Tarifa e rryma" instead of "Tarifa e rrymës" (genitive after "e").
**Why it's wrong:** After the linking article "e/i/të", the following noun must be in the genitive case.

- ❌ "Tarifa e rryma: 0.40 euro per kilovat"
- ✅ "Tarifa e rrymës elektrike: €0.40 për kilovat"

**Rule:** After "e/i/të/së" linking articles, always use the genitive form of the noun. Double-check every "e + noun" construction.

---

### MISTAKE 15: Spelling Typos ("shtpëi" for "shtëpi")
**Frequency:** 1/6 reels
**What went wrong:** Transposed letters in common words.
**Why it's wrong:** Basic spelling error that damages credibility.

- ❌ "Koston e karikimit në shtpëi"
- ✅ "Koston e karikimit në shtëpi"

**Rule:** Proofread every output for transposed letters, especially in common words: shtëpi, infrastrukturë, mirëmbajtje.

## CATEGORY 5: FORMATTING & CONSISTENCY

### MISTAKE 16: Euro Symbol Inconsistency
**Frequency:** 3/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI mixes "0.40 euro" with "€4.80" in the same script.
**Why it's wrong:** Inconsistent formatting looks unprofessional and is harder to scan.

- ❌ "0.40 euro per kilovat" then "€4.80 për 100 km"
- ✅ "€0.40 për kilovat" then "€4.80 për 100 km"

**Rule:** Pick one format (€ symbol preferred) and use it consistently throughout every piece.

---

### MISTAKE 17: Vague Vocabulary When Precision Exists
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI writes "testuar bashkë" (tested together) when "testuar si një sistem i vetëm" (tested as a single system) is available and more precise.
**Why it's wrong:** Persuasive copy requires precision to build credibility. Vague words weaken technical arguments.

- ❌ "Nuk është testuar bashkë."
- ✅ "Nuk është testuar si një sistem i vetëm."

**Rule:** When making a technical differentiation argument, use the most precise phrase available. Don't simplify when precision strengthens the case.

---

### MISTAKE 18: Missing Product Name Integration
**Frequency:** 2/6 reels
**What went wrong:** AI references "gaz fabrike" generically without naming "Clio ECO-G" or "Dacia Stepway ECO-G."
**Why it's wrong:** Product names build brand recall. Generic descriptions don't sell.

- ❌ "Makina me gaz fabrike: €4.80 për 100 km."
- ✅ "Renault Clio ECO-G. €4.80 për 100 km. Garanci 10 vjet."

**Rule:** Always integrate specific product names when available. The script is an ad — name the product.

## SUMMARY CHECKLIST

Before delivering ANY Albanian persuasive copy, verify:

- [ ] **Tone:** Is it confrontational, not educational?
- [ ] **Register:** TI form for direct audience? (Not JU unless specified)
- [ ] **Urgency:** Does it include "TANI", "Hajde", or equivalent energy markers?
- [ ] **Cold open:** Does it punch, not hypothesize?
- [ ] **Close:** Consistent branded tagline present?
- [ ] **Script vs. graphics:** Spoken word carries argument, graphics carry proof?
- [ ] **Production notes:** No filenames, spreadsheet references, or internal notes in dialogue?
- [ ] **Performance cues:** Pause, gesture, camera direction marks present?
- [ ] **Grammar:** Correct diacritics (për, ë), genitive after linking articles, present tense for habitual claims?
- [ ] **Formatting:** Consistent € symbols, number formatting, spelling?
- [ ] **Product names:** Named specifically, not generically?
- [ ] **Precision:** Technical claims use precise language, not vague approximations?
- [ ] **No dangling conditionals:** Every "Nëse" has a resolution?
