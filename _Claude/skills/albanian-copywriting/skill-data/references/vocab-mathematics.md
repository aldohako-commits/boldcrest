## LOGICAL AND PROOF VOCABULARY

### Core Terms

| Albanian | English | Context/Usage |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| **teoremë** (f.) | theorem | Rreshti: "Teoremat për rrethin" (Theorems about the circle) |
| **vërtetim** (m.) | proof | Standard term for mathematical proof |
| **provë** (f.) | proof (variant) | Less common than vërtetim; alternative form |
| **hipotezë** (f.) | hypothesis | Condition in a theorem or assumption |
| **përfundim** (m.) | conclusion | The result of reasoning or calculation |
| **prandaj** (adv.) | therefore; thus; hence | Logical connector: "X është i vërtetë, prandaj..." |
| **rrjedhim** (m.) | corollary; consequence | Direct result from a theorem |
| **pohim** (m.) | statement; claim; assertion | Mathematical claim to be proven |
| **dëshmi** (f.) | evidence; proof | Supporting reasoning |

### Proof-Related Phrases

**Pattern 1: "Të vërtetuarsh X do të thotë..."** (To prove X means...)
- Example: "Të vërtetuarsh teoremën do të thotë të tregosh se ajo është e vërtetë për të gjithë rastet."
- Translation: "To prove the theorem means to show that it is true for all cases."

**Pattern 2: "Në fillim, ne supozojmë se..."** (First, we assume that...)
- Hypothesis introduction pattern
- Sets up the condition for proof

**Pattern 3: "Prandaj, përfundojmë se..."** (Therefore, we conclude that...)
- Conclusion marking
- Signals the final step of proof

### Related Logical Terms

| Albanian | English |
|----------|---------|
| **supozim** (m.) | assumption; supposition |
| **nëse...atëherë...** | if...then... (conditional) |
| **në qoftë se** | if; in case that (formal conditional) |
| **për çdo/të gjithë** | for all; for every |
| **ekziston** | there exists |
| **është i mundshëm/e mundur** | is possible |
| **nuk është i mundshëm** | is not possible; is impossible |
| **është e nevojshme** | is necessary |
| **është e mjaftueshme** | is sufficient |

## ALGEBRAIC VOCABULARY

### Basic Terms

|----------|---------|-----------------|

| **kufizë** (f.) | term | Individual component of expression: 3x, -5, 7xy |

| **konstante** (f.) | constant | Fixed number with no variable |
| **identitet** (m.) | identity | Equation true for all values (e.g., x + x ≡ 2x) |
| **barazim** (m.) | equation | Mathematical statement with "=" sign |

### Algebraic Operations

| Albanian | English | Definition |
|----------|---------|------------|
| **reduktim** (m.) | simplification; reduction | Process of collecting like terms |

### Equations and Inequalities

| Albanian | English | Full Definition |
|----------|---------|-----------------|
| **ekuacion** (m.) | equation | Mathematical statement with "=" between two expressions |
| **ekuacion linear** | linear equation | Form: ax + b = c |
| **ekuacion i fuqisë së dytë** | quadratic equation | Form: ax² + bx + c = 0 |
| **inekuacion** (m.) | inequality | Uses symbols: <, >, ≤, ≥ |
| **zgjidhje** (f.) | solution | Value(s) that satisfy the equation/inequality |
| **të zgjidhësh** | to solve | Find all values that satisfy the equation |
| **sistem ekuacionesh** | system of equations | Multiple equations that must be solved together |

### Key Algebraic Patterns in Albanian

**Pattern: "Për të zgjidhur një ekuacion, kryeni të njëjta veprime në të dyja anët"**
- Translation: "To solve an equation, perform the same operations on both sides"
- Explains the fundamental principle of equation solving

**Pattern: "Ekuacioni i fuqisë së dytë ka trajtën ax² + bx + c = 0"**
- Translation: "A quadratic equation has the form ax² + bx + c = 0"
- Standard definition format

## ALBANIAN MATHEMATICAL NOTATION

### How Mathematical Concepts Are Expressed in Prose

#### Equations
| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **x = 5** | x equals 5 | "x ka vlerën 5" (x has the value 5) |
| **x > 5** | x is greater than 5 | "x është më i madh se 5" |
| **x < 5** | x is less than 5 | "x është më i vogël se 5" |
| **x ≥ 5** | x is greater than or equal to 5 | "x është më i madh ose i barabartë me 5" |
| **x ≤ 5** | x is less than or equal to 5 | "x është më i vogël ose i barabartë me 5" |
| **x ≡ y** | x is identical to y | "x është identik me y" |

#### Operations
| Operation | Albanian | Example |
|-----------|----------|---------|

| **-** (subtraction) | zbritje | 5 - 2 = "pesë minus dy" |
| **×** (multiplication) | shumëzim | 3 × 4 = "tre herë katër" |

## SENTENCE STRUCTURES AND EXPRESSIONS

### Definition Pattern

**Standard form**: "[Term] quhet [definition]"
- Example: "Trekëndësh barabrinjës quhet trekëndëshi i cili ka tre brinjë të barabarta."
- Translation: "An equilateral triangle is called the triangle which has three equal sides."

**Variation**: "[Term] është [definition]"
- Example: "Kënd i drejtë është këndi me masë 90°."
- Translation: "A right angle is the angle with measure 90°."

### Explanation Pattern

**Form**: "Të [verb] do të thotë [explanation]"
- Example: "Të reduktosh një shprehje do të thotë të dallosh kufizat e ngjashme."
- Translation: "To reduce an expression means to identify like terms."

This pattern is crucial for explaining processes in Albanian mathematics.

### Problem-Solving Pattern

**Step introduction**: "Hapi I:" / "Hapi II:" / "Hapi III:" (Step I, Step II, Step III)
- Creates numbered sequential instruction
- Standard in worked examples

**Example structure**:
```
SHEMBULL (Example):
Zgjidhni ekuacionin: 3x + 5 = 23
Hapi I: 3x = 23 - 5
Hapi II: 3x = 18
Hapi III: x = 6
```

### Hypothesis-Conclusion Pattern

**Form**: "Nëse [condition], atëherë [result]"
- Example: "Nëse dy brinjë të trekëndëshit janë të barabarta, atëherë këndet përballë tyre janë të barabartë."
- Translation: "If two sides of a triangle are equal, then the angles opposite them are equal."

### Question Patterns

**Direct inquiry**: "Gjeni vlerën e..."
- Example: "Gjeni vlerën e x në ekuacionin e mëposhtëm."
- Translation: "Find the value of x in the following equation."

**Reasoning request**: "Argumentoni përfundimin tuaj."
- Translation: "Justify/support your conclusion."
- Asks for proof or reasoning

**Verification**: "Vërtetoni se..."
- Translation: "Prove that..."
- Explicit proof request

### Comparative Structures

**Equality**: "është i barabartë me" (is equal to)
- Example: "Syprina e katrorit është e barabartë me a²."

**Inequality**:
- "është më i madh se" (is greater than)
- "është më i vogël se" (is less than)

**Proportionality**: "është në përpjesëtim me" (is proportional to)

### Conditional Structures for Solving

**"Për të ... kryeni të njëjta veprime në të dyja anët"**
- Translation: "To ... perform the same operations on both sides"
- Fundamental principle explanation

**"Së pari ..., më pas ..."**
- Translation: "First ..., then ..."
- Sequential instruction

### Common Solution Introductions

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Zgjidhje**: | Solution: |
| **Përgjigje**: | Answer: |
| **KUJTONI**: | REMEMBER: (Important fact) |
| **AFTËSI**: | SKILLS: (Learning objective) |
| **ZBATIM**: | APPLICATION: (Practical use) |
| **Shkathtësi**: | Skill/competence level |

## TRANSLATION TRAPS AND COMMON MISTAKES

### 1. "Vlerë" vs. "Kuptim"

| Albanian | English | **CORRECT USAGE** |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **vlerë** (f.) | value; worth | Numerical value: "vlera e x", "vlera e shifrës" |
| **kuptim** (m.) | meaning; sense | Semantic meaning: "kuptimi i fjalës" |

**TRAP**: Don't use "kuptim" when asking for numerical value.

**Example**:
- ✓ "Gjeni vlerën e x" (Find the VALUE of x)
- ✗ "Gjeni kuptimin e x" (Find the MEANING of x - incorrect)

---

### 2. "Reduktim" vs. "Thjeshtim" vs. "Zvogëlim"

| Term | English | **CORRECT USAGE** |
|------|---------|--------|
| **reduktim** | reduction; simplification | Combining like terms in algebra |
| **thjeshtim** | simplification | General simplification of expressions |
| **zvogëlim** | reduction; decrease | Making something smaller in quantity |

**TRAP**: In mathematics, "reduktim" specifically means collecting like terms in algebraic expressions.

**Example**:
- ✓ "Reduktoni shprehjen: 3x + 2x = 5x" (Reduce the expression)
- ✓ "Thjeshtoni thyesën: 6/8 = 3/4" (Simplify the fraction)
- ✗ "Zvogëloni shprehjen" (Not used in this algebraic context)

---

### 3. "Kënd" Precision

| Form | English | **TRAP** |
|------|---------|--------|
| **kënd** (m.) | angle | Basic noun |
| **kënde** | angles | Plural |
| **këndi i drejtë** | right angle | **MUST use "i drejtë" adjective** |
| **këndi i gjerë** | obtuse angle | **MUST use "i gjerë"** |
| **masë e këndit** | angle measure | **CAREFUL**: measure is feminine |

**TRAP**: Albanian adjectives must agree in gender and number. "Kënd i drejtë" (with "i") is correct; "kënd drejtë" (without "i") sounds unnatural.

---

### 4. "Drejtëz" vs. "Vijë"

| Albanian | English | **USAGE** |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **drejtëz** (f.) | straight line; line | Mathematical: straight, infinite |
| **vijë** (f.) | line; curve | General: can be curved |
| **segment** | line segment | Part of a line between two points |

**TRAP**: In mathematics, "drejtëz" refers specifically to straight infinite lines. Don't use "vijë" when you mean geometric line.

**Example**:
- ✓ "Drejtëza e përmbysjes" (Line of symmetry - must be straight)
- ✗ "Vija e përmbysjes" (Incorrect in geometry context)

---

### 5. "Brinjë" vs. "Anë"

| Albanian | English | **USAGE** |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **brinjë** (f.) | side; edge (of geometric figure) | Geometry: edges of polygons, polyhedra |
| **anë** (f.) | side; direction | General or equation sides |

**TRAP**: "Brinjë" is standard in geometry. "Anë" is used for "both sides of an equation" in context of equation solving.

**Example**:
- ✓ "Kryeni të njëjta veprime në të dyja anët e ekuacionit" (both SIDES of equation)
- ✓ "Trekëndëshi ka tre brinjë" (triangle has three EDGES/SIDES)

### 6. "Syprina" and Area Misconceptions

### Patterns with "pa" (without)

A frequent modifier in calculation exercises:

**Pattern**: "[Verb] ... **pa përdorur** [tool/method]"
- Translation: "[Verb] ... **without using** [calculator/method]"
- Usage: Restricts solution methods to mental arithmetic or paper-based calculation
- Example: "Njehsoni 5,12 – 4,92 **pa përdorur makinën llogaritëse**"
  - Translation: "Calculate 5.12 – 4.92 **without using a calculator**"
- Synonyms: "pa ndihmën e", "pa ndihmë"

### Conditional Problem Structures

#### Type A: "Në qoftë se ... atëherë ..."

**Pattern**: "Në qoftë se [condition], atëherë [find/prove X]"
- Translation: "If [condition], then [find/prove X]"
- Used in: Conditional calculation problems
- Example: "Në qoftë se vargu është progresion aritmetik, gjeni ndryshesën e përbashkët"
  - Translation: "If the sequence is an arithmetic progression, find the common difference"

#### Type B: "Për ç'vlerë të ... ?"

**Pattern**: "Për ç'vlerë të [variable], [condition]?"
- Translation: "For what value of [variable] [does condition hold]?"
- Asks for the specific input value that satisfies a given condition
- Example: "Për ç'vlerë të n-së përftohet 483 në vargun A?"
  - Translation: "For what value of n is 483 obtained in sequence A?"
- Variant: "Për cilën vlerë..." (more formal)

#### Type C: Relative Clause Conditions

**Pattern**: "[Statement] të cilin/të cilat [condition]"
- Translation: "[Statement] which [condition]"
- Specifies subset or filtered case
- Example: "Provoni që prodhimi i një numri tek me një numur çift është numur çift"
  - Translation: "Prove that the product of an odd number with an even number is an even number"

## NOTES ON GRADE 9 PROGRESSION

Grade 9 mathematics represents a transition from concrete arithmetic to more abstract algebraic and geometric thinking. Key characteristics:

1. **Sets**: Introduced as organizing principle; foundation for more advanced logic
2. **Sequences**: Arithmetic and geometric; lead to understanding of functions
3. **Percentages**: Extended to real-world applications (growth rates, discounts)
4. **Geometry**: 2D shapes formalized; introduction to 3D solid shapes
5. **Trigonometry**: Initial concepts in right triangles; preview of advanced study
6. **Probability**: From intuitive to formal; introduction of sample spaces and events
7. **Algebra**: Extension to quadratic equations; polynomials of higher degree

The vocabulary emphasizes **relationships** and **properties** rather than mere definitions, reflecting the shift toward mathematical reasoning.

## Sentence Structures in Mathematical Albanian

### Pattern 1: Imperative Instruction (Direct Command)
**Structure**: Command verb + object/parameters
**Function**: Used to direct students to perform calculations, constructions, or verifications

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Shkruani bashkësinë P të numrave natyrorë më të mëdhenj se 5 e më të vegjël se 14." — Write the set P of natural numbers greater than 5 and less than 14.
- "Llogaritni shumën e këndeve të brendshme të një pesëkëndëshi." — Calculate the sum of interior angles of a pentagon.
- "Gjeni vlerën e shprehjeve të mëposhtme për x = 3." — Find the value of the following expressions for x = 3.
- "Thjeshtoni shprehjet e mëposhtme." — Simplify the following expressions.
- "Vizatoni një diagram ku të tregohen T, T' dhe T''." — Draw a diagram showing T, T' and T''.
- "Ndërtoni një tabelë të dendurive të grumbulluara." — Build a table of cumulative frequencies.

**Common imperative verbs in mathematical contexts**:
- llogaritni (calculate)
- gjeni (find)
- provoni (prove)
- tregoni (show)
- shkruani (write)
- vizatoni (draw)
- ndërtoni (construct/build)
- thjeshtoni (simplify)
- faktorizoni (factorize)
- zbërtheni (expand)
- njehsoni (compute)

### Pattern 2: Definition with "quhet" (is called)
**Structure**: Subject + "quhet" + definition
**Function**: Introduces new mathematical terms and concepts

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Objektet që përbëjnë bashkësinë quhen elemente të bashkësisë." — The objects that make up the set are called elements of the set.
- "Një bashkësi që nuk ka element është bashkësi boshe." — A set that has no elements is called an empty set.
- "Matematik, përshkruani se si nëndahet 20 në faktorë të tij." — Mathematics: describe how 20 is divided into its factors (implied "quhet").
- "Bashkësia boshë." — The empty set (noun phrase used to name the concept).
- "Një numër i thjeshtë ka saktësisht dy faktorë: veten dhe njëshin." — A prime number has exactly two factors: itself and one (definition via property).

---

### Pattern 3: Conditional Structure ("nëse...atëherë" / "kur...ndodh")
**Structure**: "Nëse" + condition + "atëherë" + consequence
**Function**: States mathematical conditions and their results

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Në qoftë se x = 3, atëherë 2x + 7 = 13." — If x = 3, then 2x + 7 = 13.
- "Në qoftë se makina vazhdon të zhvlerësohet me të njëjtën normë, pas sa vitesh ajo do të vlejë më pak se 100 000 lekë?" — If the car continues to depreciate at the same rate, after how many years will it be worth less than 100,000 lekë?
- "Nëse numri ka më shumë se dy faktorë, atëherë ai është numër i përbërë." — If a number has more than two factors, then it is a composite number.
- "Kur d = 2,5, s = 48." — When d = 2.5, s = 48.

---

### Pattern 4: Existence and Uniqueness ("ekziston", "ka vetëm një", "nuk ekziston")
**Structure**: Quantified statement about existence of mathematical objects
**Function**: Asserts or denies the existence of solutions or elements

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Nuk ekziston numër i plotë ndërmjet 1 dhe 2." — There is no integer between 1 and 2.
- "Ka vetëm një bashkësi boshe." — There is only one empty set.
- "Disa bashkësi nuk kanë element." — Some sets have no elements.
- "Ekziston një numër të panjohur karamelesh me ngjyra të ndryshme." — There exists an unknown number of candies with different colors.

---

### Pattern 5: Comparison ("është më i madh se", "është i barabartë me", "është më i vogël se")
**Structure**: Subject + comparative form + object
**Function**: Compares mathematical quantities and values

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Albi thotë që ata kanë të njëjtën moshë nëse moshat e tyre do të rrumbullakosen në dhjetëshen më të afërt." — Albi says they have the same age if their ages are rounded to the nearest ten.
- "9 është më i madh se 6." — 9 is greater than 6.
- "Syprina e figurës është 30 cm²." — The area of the figure is 30 cm².
- "Dy drejtkëndëshat nuk janë të ngjashëm." — The two rectangles are not similar.

---

### Pattern 6: Classification/Division ("ndahet në", "mund të jetë", "ka lloje të ndryshme")
**Structure**: Subject + divisive verb + categories
**Function**: Categorizes mathematical objects and concepts

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Shumëkëndëshat ndahen në të rregullt dhe jo të rregullt." — Polygons are divided into regular and irregular.
- "Bashkësia mund të ketë shumë elemente ose sa të dojë elemente." — A set can have many elements or as few elements as you want.
- "Numrat e plotë mund të jenë pozitiv, negativ ose zero." — Integers can be positive, negative, or zero.
- "Një shndërrim mund të jetë zhvendosje paralele, rrotullim ose simetri." — A transformation can be a translation, rotation, or reflection.

---

### Pattern 7: Quantification ("për çdo", "ka të paktën", "saktësisht")
**Structure**: Quantifier + set + property
**Function**: Makes statements about all, some, or specific numbers of elements

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Për çdo bashkësi, ekziston bashkësia boshe." — For every set, there exists an empty set.
- "Ka të paktën pesë elementë në bashkësinë X." — There are at least five elements in set X.
- "Saktësisht dy faktorë ka një numër i thjeshtë." — A prime number has exactly two factors.
- "Të gjithë nxënësit duhet të zgjidhin këtë problem." — All students must solve this problem.

---

### Pattern 8: Negation ("nuk ekziston", "s'ka", "nuk është")
**Structure**: Negative particle + verb/adjective + object
**Function**: Denies the existence, equality, or truth of mathematical statements

**Examples from textbook**:
- "2 nuk është element i bashkësisë {1, 3, 5, 7, 9}." — 2 is not an element of the set {1, 3, 5, 7, 9}.
- "Ky trekëndësh nuk është barabrinjës." — This triangle is not equilateral.
- "S'ka mundësi që ky numër të jetë i thjeshtë." — It's impossible for this number to be prime.
- "Nuk mund të pjesëtohet me zero." — Cannot be divided by zero.

---

### Pattern 9: Result and Consequence ("prandaj", "nga kjo rrjedh", "si rezultat", "pra")
**Structure**: Logical connective + consequence statement
**Function**: Introduces conclusions drawn from premises in logical reasoning

**Examples from textbook**:
- "2 është element i bashkësisë {1, 2, 3}. Pra, mund të shkruajmë: 2 ∈ {1, 2, 3}." — 2 is an element of the set {1, 2, 3}. Therefore, we can write: 2 ∈ {1, 2, 3}.
- "Nëse të gjithë numrat janë pozitiv, atëherë shuma duhet të jetë pozitive. Prandaj, shuma e këtyre numrave nuk mund të jetë negative." — If all numbers are positive, then the sum must be positive. Therefore, the sum of these numbers cannot be negative.
- "Mesatarja e dhënave është 50. Si rezultat, vlera qendrore e shpërndarjes është 50." — The average of the data is 50. As a result, the central value of the distribution is 50.
- "Nga kjo rrjedh se të dy trekëndëshat janë kongruentë." — From this follows that both triangles are congruent.

### Pattern 10: Step-by-Step Solution ("Hapi 1", "Më pas", "Së fundmi", "Përfundim")
**Structure**: Sequential markers + procedural statements
**Function**: Structures problem-solving processes into ordered steps

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Hapi 1: Shkruani bashkësinë. Hapi 2: Vizatoni diagramin. Hapi 3: Emërtoni nënbashkësinë." — Step 1: Write the set. Step 2: Draw the diagram. Step 3: Name the subset.
- "Më pas, llogaritni syprinën e figurës." — Next, calculate the area of the figure.
- "Më përktheni pas zgjerimit, reduktoni shprehjen." — After expanding, reduce the expression.
- "Përfundim: Vlera e shprehjeve është 5." — Conclusion: The value of the expressions is 5.

### Pattern 11: Question Patterns (Direct Questions)
**Structure**: Question word + statement/verb
**Function**: Formulates problems and inquiries for student response

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Sa elemente ka në bashkësinë X?" — How many elements are in set X?
- "Cilat nga këto bashkësi janë bashkësi boshe?" — Which of these sets are empty sets?
- "Për ç'vlerë të x është shprehja e barabartë me zero?" — For what value of x is the expression equal to zero?
- "Cila është mesatarea e këtyre numrave?" — What is the average of these numbers?
- "Pse këta dy trekëndësha nuk janë kongruentë?" — Why are these two triangles not congruent?

### Pattern 12: Relative Clause in Definition ("numri që plotëson...", "bashkësia që ka...")
**Structure**: Head noun + relative clause with "që" (that/which)
**Function**: Refines definitions by specifying properties that objects must satisfy

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Elementi që i përket bashkësisë quhet anëtar i bashkësisë." — The element that belongs to the set is called a member of the set.
- "Numrat që mbarojnë me 0 janë numra çift." — Numbers that end with 0 are even numbers.
- "Trekëndëshi që ka të tre brinjët e barabarta quhet trekëndësh barabrinjës." — The triangle that has all three sides equal is called an equilateral triangle.
- "Bashkësia e numrave tek ndërmjet 0 dhe 10 është bashkësia që përmban 1, 3, 5, 7, 9." — The set of odd numbers between 0 and 10 is the set that contains 1, 3, 5, 7, 9.

**Structure**: Nominalized verb form (typically ending in -ja, -im) used as noun
**Function**: Refers to mathematical operations and processes as abstract nouns

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Mbledhja e dy numrave jep shumën." — The addition of two numbers gives the sum.
- "Zbritja është operacioni i kundërt i mbledhjes." — Subtraction is the inverse operation of addition.
- "Shumëzimi i dy numrave është operacion themeltar." — Multiplication of two numbers is a fundamental operation.
- "Pjesëtimi i 12 me 3 jep 4." — The division of 12 by 3 gives 4.
- "Faktorizimi i shprehjes duhet bërë me kujdes." — Factorization of the expression must be done carefully.

### Pattern 14: Explanatory Structure ("d.m.th.", "përkatësisht", "domethënë")
**Structure**: Main statement + clarifying connective + explanation
**Function**: Provides additional clarification or alternative phrasing of concepts

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Numrat çift janë 2, 4, 6, 8, ... d.m.th., numrat që plotpjesëtohen me 2." — Even numbers are 2, 4, 6, 8, ... i.e., numbers divisible by 2.
- "Rrezja e rrethit, përkatësisht gjatësia nga qendra në perimetrin e rrethit, shënohet me r." — The radius of the circle, namely the length from the center to the circumference, is denoted by r.
- "Shuma, domethënë rezultati i mbledhjes, jepet në këtë shembull." — The sum, that is, the result of addition, is given in this example.

---

### Pattern 15: Proof Structure (with "Le të jetë", "Supozojmë", "Pra")
**Structure**: Hypothesis + logical steps + conclusion
**Function**: Structures mathematical proofs and logical arguments

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Le të jetë x një element i bashkësisë X. Atëherë x gjendet në diagramin e Venit për bashkësinë X." — Let x be an element of set X. Then x appears in the Venn diagram for set X.
- "Supozojmë se dy drejtkëndëshat janë të ngjashëm. Pra, brinjët përkatëse janë proporcionale." — Suppose two rectangles are similar. Then the corresponding sides are proportional.
- "Të provohet: Shuma e këndeve të brendshme të trekëndëshit është 180°." — To be proved: The sum of interior angles of a triangle is 180°.
- "Prandaj, trekëndëshat ABC dhe XYZ janë kongruentë (q.d.d.)." — Therefore, triangles ABC and XYZ are congruent (Q.E.D.).

---

### Pattern 16: Attribution of Properties (Adjectival Description)
**Structure**: Noun + attributive adjective or relative construction
**Function**: Characterizes mathematical objects with essential and non-essential properties

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Bashkësia boshë është bashkësia që nuk ka ndonjë element." — The empty set is the set that has no elements.
- "Numrat e thjeshtë kanë saktësisht dy faktorë." — Prime numbers have exactly two factors.
- "Shndërrimet gjeometrike janë vepra që ndryshojnë pozicionin ose formën e një figure." — Geometric transformations are operations that change the position or shape of a figure.
- "Rrethi i rregullt ka të gjitha brinjët të barabarta." — A regular polygon has all sides equal.

## Grammar Context in Mathematics Texts

### 1. Gender and Grammatical Agreement in Mathematical Objects
**Observation**: Mathematical objects generally follow the standard gender patterns of Albanian nouns, though in mathematical contexts they maintain consistent gender for agreement purposes.

**Gender patterns**:
- **Feminine**: e shumta (the sum/addition) — e barabartë (equal), e mëdhezja (the large one), bashkësia (the set), formula (the formula), shprehje (the expression), syprina (the area)
- **Masculine**: numri (the number), elementi (the element), faktor (the factor), trekëndëshi (the triangle), kujtim (the memory), zmadhim (the enlargement)
- **Neuter**: (Albanian has limited neuter; mostly masculine and feminine): rrethi (the circle), ekuacion (the equation)

**Examples of agreement**:
- "Bashkësia e zbrazët" — the empty set (f. sg., adj. agrees with f. sg. noun)
- "Numri i madh" — the large number (m. sg., adj. agrees with m. sg. noun)
- "Shprehje të thjeshtë" — simplified expressions (f. pl., adj. agrees with f. pl. noun)
- "Elementet e bashkësisë" — the elements of the set (m. pl., adj./art. agrees)

---

### 2. How Formulas Are Read Aloud and Verbalized in Albanian
**Observation**: Mathematical formulas and symbolic expressions are converted to natural language following specific Albanian conventions.

**Formula reading patterns**:
- **Equations**: "2x + 7 = 13" reads as "dy x më 7 e barabartë me 13" (two x plus 7 equals 13)
- **Operations**: "x × y" reads as "x herë y" (x times y) or "x shumëzuar me y" (x multiplied by y)
- **Division**: "a ÷ b" reads as "a pjesëtuar me b" (a divided by b)
- **Powers**: "x²" reads as "x në katror" (x to the square) or "x pangris në fuqinë e dytë" (x raised to the power of two)
- **Square root**: "√x" reads as "rrënja katrore e x-it" (the square root of x)
- **Infinity**: "∞" reads as "pafundësi" (infinity)

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Shënojmë P = {6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13}" — We denote P equals the set of 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13
- "x² + 2x − 5" reads as "x në katror më dy x minus 5" (x squared plus 2x minus 5)

---

### 3. Imperative vs. Subjunctive in Mathematical Instructions
**Observation**: Mathematical instructions predominantly use the imperative mood for direct commands, while subjunctive appears in conditional scenarios and when describing what "should" be done.

**Imperative mood** (direct command):
- "Llogaritni syprinën." — Calculate the area.
- "Gjeni faktorët." — Find the factors.
- "Vizatoni diagramin." — Draw the diagram.

**Subjunctive/Conditional mood** (less direct instruction):
- "Duhet të gjeni faktorët." — You should find the factors. (conditional)
- "Mund të përdorni kalkulatorin." — You may use a calculator. (conditional permission)
- "Nëse x = 3, atëherë mund të gjeni vlerën." — If x = 3, then you can find the value. (conditional scenario)

---

### 4. Definite Article with Mathematical Objects
**Observation**: Mathematical objects typically use the definite article when referring to a specific instance but omit it when referring to the concept generally.

**With definite article** (specific reference):
- "Trekëndëshi ABC" — the triangle ABC (specific triangle)
- "Rrethi me rreze 5 cm" — the circle with radius 5 cm (specific circle)
- "Bashkësia e numrave natyrorë" — the set of natural numbers (specific set)
- "Elementi x" — the element x (when x is specifically identified)

**Without definite article** (general reference):
- "Bashkësia është një grumbull objektesh." — A set is a collection of objects. (general concept)
- "Numri i thjeshtë ka dy faktorë." — A prime number has two factors. (general property)
- "Trekëndëshi kënddrejtë ka një kënd 90°." — A right triangle has a 90° angle. (general property)

---

### 5. Preposition Usage in Mathematical Contexts
**Observation**: Specific prepositions are consistently used in mathematical Albanian to express relationships between quantities and objects.

**Common mathematical prepositions**:
- **"me" (with/by)**: Shumëzimi me 2 (multiplication by 2), një ekuacion me një ndryshore (an equation with one variable)
- **"nga" (from/of)**: Diferenca nga 10 (the difference from 10), faktori më i madh nga të gjithë (the greatest factor of all)
- **"për" (for/per)**: Për çdo numër (for every number), formula për syprinën (the formula for area)
- **"prej" (from)**: Prej 1 në 10 (from 1 to 10), raporti prej 5 në 3 (the ratio from 5 to 3)
- **"në" (in/at)**: Në bashkësinë X (in set X), në trajtën më të thjeshtë (in the simplest form)
- **"brenda" (within)**: Brenda klasës (within the class), numrat brenda 1 dhe 100 (numbers within 1 and 100)

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Numrat më të mëdhenj se 5 e më të vegjël se 14" — Numbers greater than 5 and less than 14
- "Ndërtimet me shkallë zvogëlimi" — Constructions with scale reduction
- "Shuma nga 1 deri në 100" — The sum from 1 to 100

---

### 6. Ordinal Numbers in Mathematical Language
**Observation**: Ordinal numbers follow a consistent pattern in Albanian and are used to order elements in sequences and list steps.

**Ordinal number formation**:
- i parë (1st, m. sg.), e parë (1st, f. sg.) — from "parë" (first)
- i dytë (2nd, m. sg.), e dytë (2nd, f. sg.) — from "dy" (two)
- i tretë (3rd, m. sg.), e tretë (3rd, f. sg.) — from "tre" (three)
- i katërt (4th), i pestë (5th), i gjashtë (6th), i shtatë (7th), i tetë (8th), i nëntë (9th), i dhjetë (10th)

**Usage in mathematical contexts**:
- "Termi i parë i vargut" — The first term of the sequence
- "Koordinata e dytë" — The second coordinate
- "Kusht i tretë i problemës" — The third condition of the problem
- "Krehe e katërt e një shumëkëndëshi" — The fourth side of a polygon

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Gjatë këtij kreu, nxënësi/ja" — During this chapter, the student (chapter being discussed as a unit)
- "Elementi i parë, i dytë, etj." — The first element, second element, etc.

---

### 7. Plural Forms of Mathematical Terms
**Observation**: Mathematical terms form plurals according to standard Albanian rules, with gender and number affecting both the noun and its modifiers.

**Plural formation patterns**:
- **-ë ending** (many f. nouns): bashkësi → bashkësitë (set → sets), shprehje → shprehjet (expression → expressions)
- **-ë ending** (many m. nouns): numër → numrat (number → numbers), element → elementet (element → elements)
- **-er/-ar ending** (m. nouns): faktor → faktorët (factor → factors), zmadhim → zmadhimet (enlargement → enlargements)

**Plural agreement**:
- "Elementet e bashkësisë" — the elements of the set (m. pl. + f. sg. genitive)
- "Bashkësitë boshe" — the empty sets (f. pl. + f. pl. attributive adj.)
- "Numrat çift" — the even numbers (m. pl. + m. pl. attributive adj.)
- "Shprehjet algjebrike" — the algebraic expressions (f. pl. + f. pl. attributive adj.)

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Elementet e bashkësisë Y janë 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 dhe 6." — The elements of set Y are 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6.
- "Faktorët e numrit 12 janë 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 dhe 12." — The factors of number 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 12.

---

### 8. Symbolic and Mathematical Language Integration
**Observation**: Mathematical symbols (∈, ∉, =, ≠, <, >, ⊂, ∪, ∩) are seamlessly integrated into Albanian text and are verbalized when reading aloud.

**Symbol verbalizations**:
- **∈** reads as "përket" (belongs to) or "është element i" (is an element of)
- **∉** reads as "nuk përket" (doesn't belong to)
- **=** reads as "e barabartë me" (equals)
- **≠** reads as "jo i barabartë me" (not equal to)
- **<** reads as "më i vogël se" (less than)
- **>** reads as "më i madh se" (greater than)
- **⊂** reads as "është nënbashkësi e" (is a subset of)
- **∪** reads as "bashkimi i" (union of)
- **∩** reads as "prerja e" (intersection of)

**Examples from textbook**:
- "2 ∈ {1, 2, 3}" reads as "Dy përket bashkësisë 1, 2, 3" or "Dy është element i bashkësisë 1, 2, 3"
- "5 ∉ {1, 3, 7}" reads as "Pesë nuk përket bashkësisë 1, 3, 7"
- "3 < 7" reads as "Tri më i vogël se shtatë"

---

### 9. Relative Clause Structures in Mathematical Definitions
**Observation**: Relative clauses introduced by "që" (that/which) are fundamental to mathematical definitions, allowing properties to be stated concisely.

**Relative clause patterns**:
- "Numri që plotëson këtë ekuacion..." — The number that satisfies this equation...
- "Bashkësia që përmban të gjithë elementet..." — The set that contains all elements...
- "Drejtëza që kalon nëpër dy pika..." — The line that passes through two points...
- "Trekëndëshi që ka tre brinjë të barabarta..." — The triangle that has three equal sides...

**Examples from textbook**:
- "Numrat tek ndërmjet 0 dhe 10, {1, 3, 5, 7, 9} - bashkësia që përmban të gjithë numrat tek në këtë interval" — Odd numbers between 0 and 10, {1, 3, 5, 7, 9} - the set that contains all odd numbers in this interval.
- "Elementi x i bashkësisë është objekti që i përket asaj bashkësie." — The element x of the set is the object that belongs to that set.

---

### 10. Use of Nominalization in Mathematical Discourse
**Observation**: Mathematical operations and actions are frequently converted into noun forms (nominalization) to discuss them as abstract concepts.

**Common nominalizations**:
- **mbledhja** (addition) from "mblidh" (add)
- **zbritja** (subtraction) from "zbret" (subtract)
- **shumëzimi** (multiplication) from "shumëzoj" (multiply)
- **pjesëtimi** (division) from "pjesëtoj" (divide)
- **faktorizimi** (factorization) from "faktorizoj" (factorize)
- **thjeshtimi** (simplification) from "thjeshtoj" (simplify)
- **zbërthimi** (expansion) from "zbërthen" (expand)
- **llogaritja** (calculation) from "llogarit" (calculate)
- **zgjidhja** (solution) from "zgjidh" (solve)
- **vlerësimi** (evaluation) from "vlerësoj" (evaluate)

**Usage in context**:
- "Mbledhja e numrave është operacion themeltar." — Addition of numbers is a fundamental operation.
- "Faktorizimi i shprehjes duhet bërë me kujdes." — Factorization of the expression must be done carefully.
- "Zgjidhja e ekuacionit jep x = 5." — The solution of the equation gives x = 5.
- "Thjeshtimi i fraksionit e bën atë më të qartë." — Simplification of the fraction makes it clearer.

---

### Summary of Key Grammatical Patterns

**Structural characteristics of Albanian mathematical language**:
1. Heavy use of imperative mood for direct instruction
2. Consistent use of definite articles with specific mathematical objects
3. Strategic use of relative clauses ("që") for precise definitions
4. Nominalization of operations to discuss abstract mathematical processes
5. Specific preposition selection for relational concepts (me, nga, për, prej, në)
6. Ordinal numbers for sequencing and ordering
7. Gender and number agreement across all modifiers
8. Integration of symbolic notation with natural language
9. Conditional structures for hypothesis and consequence statements
10. Clear logical connectives (prandaj, pra, si rezultat) for deductive reasoning

These patterns create a precise, systematic mathematical discourse that is characteristic of Albanian mathematical texts.
