### What was learned

**From Abrashi (2011) — Albanian article (nyja) system:**
- The postpositive article is a fused case+definiteness marker — structural, not optional
- Three definiteness levels (definite/indefinite/bare) each carry distinct meaning for copy
- Article mandatory on subjects, superlatives, full-clause nouns — no creative omission allowed
- Demonstrative vs article = urgency/pointing vs familiarity/shared knowledge (useful for CTA vs brand copy)
- Article usage is itself a register signal: full usage = educated/formal; bare nouns = casual/oral

**From Drejtshkrimi (1972/1974) — Official Albanian Orthography:**
- Phonetic principle primary, morphological secondary — Albanian is spelled as pronounced
- 36-letter alphabet with digraphs treated as single letters (dh, gj, ll, nj, rr, sh, th, xh, zh)
- Extensive foreign word adaptation rules: -tion → -cion (with ks); eks- → ekz- before vowels
- Genitive article system (i, e, te, se) must match gender of governing noun — most error-prone area
- Albanian capitalizes far less than English: days, months, seasons, nationalities all lowercase
- Orthographic correctness is a strong register/credibility signal in Albanian

**From Gramatika Praktike (2007) — Comprehensive Albanian Practical Grammar:**
- Five noun cases (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, ablative)
- Five verb moods: indicative (claims), imperative (CTAs), subjunctive (suggestions), optative (wishes/greetings), admirative (surprise)
- Imperative forms are naturally short — single words work for CTAs: Zbulo. Shiko. Bli. Merr. Provo.
- Many common CTA verbs have IRREGULAR imperative forms (marr→merr, dal→dil, flas→fol, pres→prit)
- The optative mood is uniquely Albanian — critical for greetings, celebrations, seasonal campaigns
- Four negation particles (nuk, s', mos, jo) — each at a different register level; s' is punchiest for ads
- Elliptical/fragment sentences are native Albanian form — tagline fragments are expected, not errors

**From Newmark (1982) — Standard Albanian Reference Grammar:**
- Complete particle system for modulating emphasis and tone — no English equivalent exists
- The deictic particle **ja** ("here/look") is a natural attention-grabber for copy openings
- Softening particles (pa, le, para) turn aggressive imperatives into warm suggestions
- Dubitative particles (valle, ndoshta, kushedi) create curiosity and wonder
- The admirative mood expresses genuine surprise at discovery — "wow" moments English can't produce
- SA + adjective = exclamatory construction: "SA bukur!" (HOW beautiful!)

**From Spoken Albanian (1997) — Conversational course:**
- Object clitics (e, i, u, me, te, na, ju) are MANDATORY in natural Albanian — their absence is the #1 marker of non-native text
- "Duke" + participle creates flowing, cinematic progressive action for brand storytelling
- Albanian word order fronts the emphasized element — put the key benefit FIRST
- **Besa** (sworn word/pledge) is the most powerful word for brand trust in Albanian
- Commercial register has its own natural warmth: *C'deshironi?* → *Urdhëroni!*
- 7 domain vocabulary lists extracted: hospitality, wedding, shopping, sports, honor/culture, kinship, food/dining

### What was learned

**From Drejtshkrimi 1973 Expanded (compared to 1972 edition):**
- Official function titles ALWAYS lowercase ("ministri", "presidenti") — critical for brand voice consistency
- Apostrophe/clitic contractions mandatory in natural text (t'i, t'ua, m'u, s', ç') — their absence is a non-native marker
- Proper noun capitalization uses only initial letter except for compound names
- Em dash (—) for time ranges/geographic ranges, NOT hyphens
- Digraph syllabification rules prevent splitting of dh, gj, ll, nj, rr, sh, th, xh, zh

**From Ferma e Kafshëve (literary translation):**
- Narrative tense switching (imperfect for atmosphere, past definite for action) creates momentum
- "Duke" + participle is the cinematic action construction in Albanian
- Complex clitic chains (t'i, t'ua, s'po, ia) are hallmarks of native prose
- Dialogue attribution verb variety (thirri, ulëriti, klithi) creates emotional coloring
- Literary connectors (sidoqoftë, përkundrazi, mirëpo) elevate tone beyond news register
- Rich sensory vocabulary (zallamahi, katrahurë, trullosur) serves as emotional spice

**From Rregullat e Gotit (crime translation):**
- English crime/legal terms need Albanian equivalents (aktgjykim, paraburgim, dëshmitar bashkëpunues)
- Proper noun Albanization follows predictable rules (Xh for J, Ç for Ch, doubled r for r-heavy names)
- Translated dialogue should use Albanian slang idioms (bir kurve for "rat," qen for loyal soldier)
- Crime/institutional register is highly specialized and not interchangeable with general vocabulary
- The book's 12 Leadership Rules demonstrate consistent imperative + reason structure for advice copy

**From Ben Blushi's Shqipëria (original literary novel):**
- Biblical/mythological register (Zoti, fati, mëkati, shpëtimi) carries deep spiritual weight
- Extended philosophical monologue is native to Albanian literary prose — don't simplify
- Village/rural vocabulary (fshati, gruaja, burri) is poetic, not archaic
- Symbolic language (breshka, ishull, det, fortesë) creates cultural resonance impossible to translate
- Kinship terms (babai, nëna, fëmijë) carry emotional charge that must be preserved
- Nature vocabulary (det, mal, diell, hënë, yll) functions as emotional shorthand
- Military metaphors are standard for describing passion, achievement, competition
- Antithesis (dashuri/urrejtje, liri/burgim) and enumeration/anaphora are power rhetorical tools
- 11 distinct vocabulary subsystems (emotional, philosophical, identity, kinship, nature, symbolic, etc.)
- Internal monologue voice (first-person philosophical musing) is a powerful narrative mode

**From Gjuha Shqipe 5 (comprehensive grammar textbook - Session 10):**
- Three noun declension classes (Class I masculine -i, Class II masculine -u, Class III feminine -a/-ja) with 6 cases each — must be memorized
- Masculine plural spelling: -nj vs -j endings are NON-PREDICTABLE (lepuj NOT lepunj, bij NOT binj) — cannot guess
- Definite adjective agreement (i/e/të/se) must cascade across gender, number, and case simultaneously
- Personal pronoun clitics (e, i, u, me, te, na, ju) are MANDATORY in natural text — their absence is cardinal error
- Five conjugation classes with critical irregular verbs: marr→merr→mor, dal→dol, jap→dha, shoh→pashë (suppletive)
- Optative mood is unique to Albanian — critical for wishes/blessings: "Gëzuar!" "Paç fat!" "Mirë të jesh!"
- Word stress is PHONEMIC — changes meaning (barí grass vs bari of-grass, stolí accusative-table vs stoli nominative)
- Geg vs Tosk dialectal vocabulary: Standard Albanian uses Tosk forms (qumësht not tambël, fshat not katund, gjalpë not tlyn)
- Phraseological expressions are FIXED units — must never translate literally (var buzët ≠ "hang lips," means "wait expectantly")
- Traditional proverbs collection (organized by theme: work, family, trust, fate, money, wisdom, love) — valuable for authentic copy
- Complete phonology (36 phonemes, 7 vowels, 29 consonants classified as voiced/voiceless), orthography rules, semantics (synonyms/antonyms/homonyms)

**From Gjuha Shqipe 6 (advanced grammar textbook - Session 11):**
- **Subjunctive mood (mënyra lidhore) ABSOLUTELY REQUIRED** — new 4-tense system (present/imperfect/perfect/pluperfect) mandatory after modal verbs (mund, duhet, dua), purpose clauses (për të, me qëllim të), conditions, uncertainties. Missing subjunctive is THE #1 non-native marker.
- **Compound sentences entirely NEW concept** — two types: coordinate (equal clauses with e, por, kurse, prandaj) vs subordinate (dependent clauses with kur, se, nëse, megjithëse, etc.). Coordinate creates balance; subordinate creates sophistication/depth.
- **Apposition (ndajshtimi) — NEW sentence member** — two nouns referring to same entity with CASE AGREEMENT. "Arben, inxhinier-i" (nominative) vs "Arben-it, inxhinier-it" (dative). Requires proper punctuation (commas/dashes for descriptive; no punctuation for restrictive).
- **Vowel ë retention system — 9 critical rules** — wrong ë instantly marks non-native text. Rule: -ë drops before -t plural (qen→qentë), -ë keeps in genitive chains (e shtëpisë), adverbial forms (-isht), preposition contractions (n'ujë). Must memorize all 9.
- **Six verb moods complete system** — added habitual/admirative mood expressing surprise/wonder ("Sa larg qenkan!" not just descriptive statements). This is unique to Albanian and gives copy emotional depth.
- **Eight indicative tenses in detail** — present, imperfect, past definite, present perfect, past perfect, past remote, simple future, future perfect. Narrative power: imperfect (background/atmosphere) + past definite (action/event) creates momentum.
- **Advanced conjunctions — 4 coordinate + 9 subordinate types** — coordinate additive (e, edhe), disjunctive (ose), adversative (por, kurse), conclusive (prandaj); subordinate completive (se), relative (që), temporal (kur), locative (ku), causal (sepse), final (me qëllim të), manner (si), conditional (nëse), consequential (kështu që), concessive (megjithëse).
- **Ten literary devices as native persuasion tools** — metaphor (metaforë), simile (krahasim), personification (personifikimi), antithesis (antiteza), hyperbole (hiperbola), epithet (epiteti), alliteration (aliteracioni), anaphora (anafora), rhetorical questions (pyetje retorike), artistic repetition (përsëritja artistike). These are EXPECTED in elevated copy and trigger cultural/emotional resonance.
- **Advanced phonetics — consonant classification** — liquid/sonorant (l, ll, j), nasal (m, n, nj), vibrant (r, rr), voiced/voiceless distinction affects loanword adaptation and pronunciation guides.
- **Advanced orthography — sentence intonation patterns** — declarative (level pitch), interrogative (pitch rises), imperative (dramatic variation). Native Albanians "hear" word order rhythm — fronted elements feel urgent; standard order feels neutral.

**2026-03-26 — @armandohidi fitness content plan (first client revision):**
This was the skill's first real-world test. A native Albanian speaker corrected an AI-generated Instagram content plan (5 Reels + 5 Sliders for a fitness personal trainer). The corrections revealed systematic weaknesses in:
1. **Grammar basics** — "Jam X vjet" instead of "Kam X vjet", missing "herë" for frequency, wrong verb agreement (s'është ti → s'je ti)
2. **Vocabulary choices** — AI picked dictionary-correct but unnatural words (njëjtë→barabartë, zakone→shprehi, orare→axhend, progresion→progres)
3. **Idiom deficit** — AI used literal descriptions where Albanian has idiomatic expressions (prapa→pas dore, karriera po ecën→në maja, diçka që shembet→shtëpi pa themele)
4. **Abstraction tendency** — AI described problems abstractly ("dietë strikte") where Albanian speakers use vivid concrete imagery ("ha 5 bajame dhe fle 4 orë në natë")
5. **Over-complex CTAs** — AI produced long, multi-clause CTAs; native speaker stripped them to minimum
6. **Missing emphasis particles** — "kot" (in vain), "më" (anymore), qualifiers like "aq sa mundesh" (as much as you can)
All corrections documented in Traps 51-60 and §1.15 of the copy guide.
